SIX-STAR LIONESSES: England Women Crush Ukraine 6-1 in World Cup Qualifier

LIVE UPDATE ● 14:30 GMT

England Women opened their World Cup qualifying campaign with a ruthless 6-1 demolition of Ukraine away from home.

England made a massive statement on the road, silencing the home crowd with a clinical display of attacking football. While Ukraine managed to find a consolation goal through Kalinina in the 58th minute, they were simply no match for the European champions' relentless pressure. The floodgates opened immediately after the restart. Alessia Russo proved why she is one of the world's most feared strikers, netting twice in just four minutes (47', 51') to break the game wide open. Ukraine briefly threatened a comeback when Kalinina pounced on a defensive lapse to make it 2-1, but the response from the visitors was instant. Georgia Stanway took total control of the game's tempo as Ukraine's structure began to crumble. She clinically dispatched a penalty in the 64th minute before firing home a powerful second from the edge of the box just six minutes later in the 70th. The midfield dominance allowed England to maintain 70% possession throughout the final half-hour. Late substitute Jess Park put the finishing touches on the victory, scoring twice in the final twelve minutes (78', 89') to ensure the scoreline reflected England’s total superiority. The result puts England top of their qualifying group and serves as a warning to their upcoming opponents.

The Manager's View

Speaking to the press after the final whistle, the manager praised the squad's professionalism in a difficult away environment:

"We have watched this group grow for a long time. Their energy and vision today transformed how we attacked this game, and starting the qualification process with six goals away from home is exactly the intent we wanted to show."
